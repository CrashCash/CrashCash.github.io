Facts

  • The KSP wiki is worth reading.
  • Synchronous orbit for Kerbin is 2863.33km. It's easier to get a 6 hour (1 Kerbal day) orbital period than to try to hit this value exactly.
  • Kerbal days are 6 hours long. Kerbal years are 426 Kerbal days. That's 106.5 Earth days, or 3.5 Earth months, so there are 3.43 Kerbal years in an Earth year.
  • MechJeb has no problem with locked staging, so be safe and hit right-shift-L immediately after launch.
  • Saved games are in their own subdirs under the "saves" directory. There are backups in the "Backup" directory of each game.
  • The atmosphere of Kerbin goes up to 70km.
  • To raise apoapsis, burn prograde at periapsis. Likewise, to raise periapsis, burn prograde at apoapsis.
  • When you're doing a planetary transfer, ALWAYS do a fine-tune after you exit the current SOI. Set the approach distance as high as possible for a high orbit where you can cheaply do plane changes.
  • When undocking, use Smart-A.S.S. to orient radial minus to prevent recontact. Turn off Smart-A.S.S. before undocking.
  • Use the CG indicator in the VAB to put the RCS as close to the CG as possible. To test your RCS setup, go to the launch pad and use the debug menu to "hack gravity" which turns it off. You can then fire your RCS translation thrusters and see if you get any unwanted rotational coupling without having to go all the way into orbit.
  • Use seperatrons firing backwards as retrorockets to deorbit used stages. Put them in the same stage as the decoupler.
  • To transfer fuel, right-shift-right-click the fuel tanks. You should get little quantity windows with In/Out buttons.
    You can't transfer across a heatshield with "Resource Transfer Obeys Crossfeed Rules" in effect.
  • When a kerbal is on a ladder, shift-WASD makes him look (and jump) in that direction.
  • If you ever plan on using parachutes more than once (such as landing on Duna, then landing back at Kerbin), make an action group for "deploy chutes". Once you repack a chute, the previous staging won't ever work again. Doing a save/reload will sometimes put your re-packed parachutes back in the staging sequence.
  • If you alt-click to copy a strut, the strut you place down will automatically connect in the same way that the original strut was connected. It also works with fuel lines.
  • Using a couple of lights near your docking port to illuminate the port you are docking to is very handy when you are rendezvousing on the dark side of planets/moons.
  • Disable the front brakes. This will prevent a rover from flipping over when coming to a stop.
  • To remove symmetry from a part, remove the "sym = foo" lines in the .craft file
  • For interplanetary transfers, use an initial orbit of 650km so you can warp as fast as possible. Or just warp in the tracking station or space center.
  • Put a thermometer on everything! It doesn't cost anything and grabs those wacky science contracts.

FVWM workaround to release function keys

The FVWM config line to release defined function keys doesn't work as documented. The man page says: "A '--' action indicates that the event should be propagated to the specified window to handle." but that isn't true. Any already-defined keys are executed instead, so none of the in-game function keys work. I stumbled on the workaround entirely by chance.

Place the following in your ~/.fvwm/config file:

Key (Kerbal*Program) F1 W A FuncUseless
Key (Kerbal*Program) F2 W A FuncUseless
Key (Kerbal*Program) F3 W A FuncUseless
Key (Kerbal*Program) F4 W A FuncUseless
Key (Kerbal*Program) F5 W A FuncUseless
Key (Kerbal*Program) F6 W A FuncUseless
Key (Kerbal*Program) F7 W A FuncUseless
Key (Kerbal*Program) F8 W A FuncUseless
Key (Kerbal*Program) F9 W A FuncUseless
Key (Kerbal*Program) F10 W A FuncUseless
Key (Kerbal*Program) F11 W A FuncUseless

DestroyFunc FuncUseless
AddToFunc FuncUseless
+ I Echo FuncUseless

... and no, I have no idea why it works. FuncUseless gets called, but it does NOT consume the keystroke.

Flight director (8-ball) symbols

  • Prograde (yellow, dotted center) forward along orbit
  • Retrograde (yellow, crossed center) backward along orbit
  • Normal (purple triangle, plain) out of orbit plane, northbound
  • Anti-normal (purple triangle, arms out) out of orbit plane, southbound
  • Radial-out (aqua circle, arms out) directly away from planet
  • Radial-in (aqua circle, arms in) toward planet
  • Maneuver node thrust direction (blue tri-arm) with a blue arrow pointing to it, if it's not visible. The amount of ΔV is indicated in a green bar with a burn time display. There's also a countdown to the node encounter. It's best to cross the node in the middle of the burn.
  • Target (purple broken circle) direction of target
  • Anti-target (purple dot, arms out) direction away from target

Maneuver nodes

  • "Spikies" (handles) symbols have the same meaning as the nav ball symbols.
  • Unselected nodes appear as small brown asterisks
  • The node adds a brown (or purple for a second node) projected trajectory path to the map when you pull/push any of the handles. Pulling the handles represent a burn in that direction. You can use the mouse scroll wheel for finer adjustments.
  • Maneuver nodes add a blue target to the attitude ball, or a blue arrow if it's not visible.
  • To delete a node, right-click the center circle and click the red close button that appears.
  • You can drag nodes along your trajectory.
  • When landing on bodies with no atmosphere, whack a maneuver node at ground level and adjust it fully retrograde until the resultant is zero. You can suicide burn every time, and not have to worry about fuel wastage. Or you can use the "BetterBurnTime" mod.
  • A perigee marker does not show up until you have a perigee above the surface.

Orbital maneuvers in the dark

  • High-latitude moon mission from Woomerang:
    • Launch into a low 75km orbit. You'll end up in a 45° inclined orbit.
    • Create a node and target the moon you're interested in.
    • Open the maneuver node editor and use "Snap node to AN/DN with target". Pick whichever one is closest to an intercept.
    • Push the apogee out to the target's orbit.
    • Use the "+o" to advance orbits until you get an intercept.
    • Tab to focus the target. You'll notice you will end up in a polar orbit of the target.
    • Back off the prograde ΔV until the orbit is just inside the target's SOI. This will make the upcoming inclination change as cheap as possible.
    • Make sure you have Kerbal Alarm Clock installed. Go to the space center and warp until your maneuver. You don't want to wait for the slow low-altitude warp.
    • After the injection burn, create a "burn to fine-tune approach" at 750km, use the radial+ button to make the final orbit as low-inclination as possible, then add to the prograde burn to raise the inital periapsis.
    • You want to make the initial orbit as high as possible, to conserve fuel and make the final plane change as cheap as possible.
  • Equatorial orbits from non-equatorial launch sites:
    • Make a dogleg during ascent. Basically you yaw some during ascent, making a "dogleg" or kink in your ascent trajectory. You can use this to change your ascent azimuth, and thus inclination; it is, however, more useful for increasing than decreasing inclination, since the impetus for your inclination comes from gravity's pull due to your offset from the equator, and thus builds throughout your ascent until you reach the equator.
    • For geostationary launches: launch into a parking orbit, then insert into geostationary transfer orbit at the lowest AN/DN. This will ensure you arrive at geosynchronous height at the DN/AN (respectively) and can thus combine plane change with your apogee kick (circularization). This is more efficient than circularizing and then performing the plane change, since (1) you burn along the hypotenuse of the triangle rather than two sides (and thus escape cosine losses) and (2) compared to a plane change after apogee kick, you are at a lower orbital velocity and thus the plane change is cheaper. It is for that reason also much, much cheaper than making a plane change burn in your parking orbit.
    • If launching from very high latitudes (the break-even point is about 45 degrees IIRC), you can launch into a super-synchronous orbit and perform a bi-elliptic transfer. You actually burn for a much higher apogee, and do your plane change there (cost of higher apogee + cheaper plane change + circularization burn to bring your apogee down < more expensive plane change). Then you move your perigee to the desired orbit's apogee, and finally you circularize at perigee.
    • Launched a Kerbal X from Woomerang into 77x72 orbit at 45.3 degrees, leaving 2,473 units of fuel, then saved game.
      Mission: 1,000 km equatorial orbit using minimum fuel
      1. Plane change, then burn to final orbit - 45 units fuel left (2,428 consumed)
      2. Burn to final orbit apoapsis at lowest AN/DN with a plane change, then circularize - 168 units fuel left (2,305 consumed)
      3. Burn to final orbit, then plane change - 405 units fuel left (2,068 consumed)

Career

  • To add funds, edit the "funds =" line in the SFS save-files, or use the "Funding" buttons in the debug menu cheat screen.
  • To unlock a part, change the "unlockTechs" and "TechRequired" to "start", and set all the "entryCost" entries to "0" in the part.cfg, then go to the R&D center and research them all.
  • For contracts, "expires" means how long the contract is available to accept, and "duration" is how long you have to complete the requirements.
  • The "enter orbit" contracts have a minimum orbital altitude. If this hasn't fired, raise your orbit.
  • After you enter orbit, look for a "science in orbit" contract, even if you already have an "explore" contract which includes science in orbit. It's nice to get paid twice.
  • After landing, grab a "plant flag" contract (and a flag!) before EVA.
  • To rescue a kerbal in orbit, use the Klaw on his pod, and transfer him over. This avoids EVA.
  • If you have to do something in an "area" or "site" then you can click the mapview icon to "start navigation" which will give you a direction indicator on the nav-ball, and tell you when you're entering the site.
  • When putting satellites in specific orbits, pay attention to the orbit direction arrows!
  • To upgrade all facilities, use the "maximum facility" button in the debug menu cheat screen.

      • Hohmann Transfers Replaced with Bi-Impulsive Transfers
        So as a "weekend project" I replaced the Hohmann Transfer planner to turn it into a proper bi-impulsive optimal transfer planner (the generalized Hohmann transfer problem).
        This is bi-impulsive -- not bi-elliptical -- the bi-elliptical transfer is actually tri-impulsive. There are algorithms to refine a bi-impulsive transfer into an optimal N-impulse trajectory, which this could feed into, but this is not that.
        The default behavior if you have circular, coplanar orbits is that you just whack "create node" and it acts like the old Hohmann transfer planner, only it won't miss if you're off by a bit. But it will also plan optimal burns between non-coplanar / non-elliptical orbits. So you can launch into an equatorial parking orbit around Kerbin and plan a transfer to Minmus and it will actually hit Minmus now. It will even plan burns when one of the targets is hyperbolic. That means if you have a ship in a parking orbit and want to do an asteroid capture mission you should just be able to select the target and whack "create node" and it'll work and give you the optimum transfer.
        The "intercept only" switch is the sort of "mono-impulsive" case where you don't care about the cost of the second burn. So if you're going to lob a nuke or kinetic interceptor at something, this is what you want.
        The drag down also allows selecting the time of the burn via all the familiar MJ options for selecting the time of a burn. In that case no optimization is done of the departure time and it is fixed.
        A near-future addition to this will probably be allowing textbox entry of transfer windows for the min departure time, max departure time and max transfer time. But I hit my limit this weekend.
        A word on what is going on under the hood is that this is a Hard™ problem in mathematics to solve. There are potentially very many basins involved in this problem (consider a porkchop plot with potentially a hundred blue basins) and picking the right one, FAST is Hard™. What I threw together uses a simulated annealing global optimization first pass. This is a stochastic, random algorithm that attempts to search out the space randomly, not always necessarily taking optimum steps, but slowly "cooling" towards taking small steps to better positions. Then there is a second pass where the output of the annealing algorithm is fed into Levenburg-Marquardt to just do a gradient search for the best solution in the selected basin.
        TL;DR: Random searching is involved. You may get slightly different results each time you compute the trajectory. Generally these are off by a single orbit in the source or destination orbit, and typically will not be off by more than a few percent in cost. That isn't really a bug (although its possible that further refinement could reduce the jumps). A standard-looking Hohmann transfer I've found to be reliable. If you see roughly circular, coplanar transfers jump around a lot that would be bad.

DLC Missions

  1. Dawn of the Space Age:
    The first mission pounds into you the fact you need to tweak the fuel in the solid rocket motor to come in under the weight limit.
    You get only one chance at designing the Jebnik 1, since you can't go back to the VAB. This is apparently a bug.
  2. Trouble in the Void:
    An RCS-only rendezvous followed by a pain-in-the-ass EVA, followed by a couple precision landings.
  3. To the Mün via Minmus:
    You don't have reaction wheels. Set orbit for 98km. Turn off AoA limit. Turn off auto-warp. Set Utilities to auto-stage. Set Smart-Ass to drive to maneuver node. Set Maneuver Planner to circularize. After the first shutdown, turn off ascent guidance, disable the main engine, create a circularization node, then make Smart-ASS use the verniers for attitude control, then re-enable the engine for circularization, then disable it again. It takes some serious control dancing. Once you get to orbit, you'll have reaction wheels.
    After you're in orbit, enable hibernation on the buried probe core, otherwise you'll run out of electricity.
    You'll need to separate the probe, otherwise it doesn't count as destroyed.
    For the Mün launch, you need to launch in-plane? I've not gotten that far.
  4. Meet Me in Zero G:
    Turn off the upper vernier engines on the Agena before launch. They just drive MechJeb into oscillation.
    After that, it's two standard orbits, a rendezvous, docking, and landing. Transfer fuel before undocking.
  5. Blue Mün:
    You gotta build things. Nope.
  6. Münar 1:
    You gotta build things. Nope.
  7. Acapello 15:
    You gotta build things. Nope.

KSP file tree

This is the directory structure under KSP_linux

KSP_linux
├── GameData - user mods are installed here
│   ├── Squad
│   │   ├── Agencies - contract agency definitions
│   │   ├── Contracts - contract configuration
│   │   │   └── Icons - the icons you see for contract locations in the map view
│   │   ├── Controls - preset control layouts
│   │   ├── Experience - Pilot/Engineer/Scientist/Tourist skill config
│   │   ├── Flags - mission flag images
│   │   ├── FX - engine exhaust effects
│   │   ├── Interiors
│   │   │   └── Administration - images for the admin building background
│   │   ├── KSPedia - KSPedia pages
│   │   ├── Localization - translation strings
│   │   ├── MenuProps - ???
│   │   ├── PartList
│   │   │   └── SimpleIcons - icons used in the technology tree
│   │   ├── Parts - all the stock parts with subdirs by type (e.g. Aero, Command, Engine)
│   │   ├── Props - all the GUI elements, such as the nav ball and altimeter
│   │   ├── Resources - various .cfg files
│   │   ├── Sounds - .wav files
│   │   ├── Spaces - vehicle internal views
│   │   ├── Strategies - admin building strategies & icons
│   │   └── Tutorials - tutorial mission configs
│   └── SquadExpansion
│       └── MakingHistory - all the bits for the mission editor, premade missions, and new expansion parts
├── KSP_Data - all the packed Unity game engine stuff
├── Launcher_Data - all the packed Unity game engine stuff for the launcher
├── saves - directories for game saves, scenarios, training, plus the expansion premade missions
│   └── career - persistent.sfs and all other .sfs files
│       ├── Backup - backups of .sfs files when they're overwritten
│       └── Ships
│           ├── SPH - aircraft .craft files
│           └── VAB - rocket .craft files
├── Screenshots - screenshots taken with F1
├── Ships - premade rockets/aircraft and thumbnails
└── thumbs - PNG thumbnails of player-made ships
