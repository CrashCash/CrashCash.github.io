Motorcycle Timeline

  • 1885
    • First motorcycle - the Petroleum Reitwagen, built by Gottlieb Daimler and Wilhelm Maybach
  • 1894
    • First production motorcycle - the Hildebrand & Wolfmüller
  • 1896
    • Excelsior Motor Company produces its first motorcycle
  • 1898
    • Norton is founded
  • 1901
    • Royal Enfield produces its first motorcycle
    • Indian produces its first motorcycle
  • 1902
    • Ariel produces its first motorcycle
    • Triumph produces its first motorcycle
    • Norton produces its first motorcycle
  • 1903
    • George A. Wyman crosses the US on a California Motor Company "motor-bicycle" in 50 days
    • Husqvarna produces its first motorcycle
    • Harley-Davidson Motor Company produces its first motorcycle
  • 1905
    • Fabrique Nationale de Herstal FN Four features the first shaft drive
  • 1907
    • Excelsior founded
    • First Isle of Man TT (Tourist Trophy) race
  • 1908
    • Husqvarna sells 14 motorcycles
  • 1909
    • Sears and Roebuck Co starts selling the Auto-Cycle
    • Husqvarna sells 23 motorcycles
  • 1910
    • BSA produces its first motorcycle
  • 1911
    • Isle of Man TT race moves to the Snaefell Mountain Course
  • 1912
    • Schwinn purchases Excelsior
    • An Excelsior is first motorcycle to be officially timed at a speed of 100 mph
    • First white lines on roads
  • 1914
    • British physician Dr. Eric Gardner commissions the construction of the first helmet for Brooklands racers
    • Dr, Gardner's helmets made compulsory for the Isle of Man TT races
    • First stop signal
  • 1916
    • Sears stops selling motorcycles
  • 1919
    • Birmingham Small Arms Company Limited (BSA) produces its first motorcycle
  • 1920
    • Harley-Davidson became the largest manufacturer with their motorcycles being sold by dealers in 67 countries
  • 1923
    • BMW produces its first motorcycle
  • 1928
    • Phillip Vincent buys HRD Motors to found Vincent Motorcycles
    • Bessie Stringfield, a 5-foot-2 black woman, starts touring the country on her Harley - owned 27 Harleys in her lifetime
    • First front-wheel brakes on Harley-Davidson motorcycles met with skepticism
  • 1931
    • Ariel produces the Square Four, designed by Edward Turner
  • 1935
    • BMW R12 & R17 are the first with hydraulically damped telescopic forks
    • T.E. Lawrence dies from head injuries after crashing his Brough Superior SS100
    • Stanley Woods wins the Senior TT on a Moto Guzzi 500 with rear suspension
  • 1937
    • First Daytona 200
  • 1939
    • First flashing turn signals
  • 1940
    • The Motor Maids club is founded
    • First sealed-beam headlights
  • 1941
    • Australian neurosurgeon Dr. Hugh Cairns, who attended T.E. Lawrence, publishes "Head Injuries in Motorcyclists - the importance of the crash helmet" in the British Medical Journal, which is the first study of the subject
  • 1945
    • 198,000 motorcycles registered in the US
  • 1946
    • Piaggio introduces the Vespa
  • 1948
    • Sears sells Cushman scooters as Allstates
    • The Hells Angels are founded
    • Honda is founded
    • Vincent Black Lightning is the world's fastest production motorcycle with Rollie Free doing 150.313mph at the Bonneville Salt Flats
  • 1949
    • Rex McCandless designs the Norton "featherbed" double-loop frame
    • The first Grand Prix championship
    • Honda produces its first motorcycle
  • 1950
    • Ducati produces its first motorcycle
    • Ducati under government Istituto per la Ricostruzione Industriale (IRI) management
  • 1951
    • Sears sells Vespa scooters as Allstates
    • BSA Group buys Ariel and Triumph, becomes the largest manufacturer
  • 1952
    • Suzuki produces the "Power Free"
  • 1953
    • Patent for protective helmet issued to University of Southern California Professor C. F. "Red" Lombard for energy-absorbing liner separate from the comfort padding
    • "The Wild One" released, with Marlon Brando riding a Triumph
  • 1954
    • Sears sells Puch motorcycles as Allstates
    • Bell produces the "500TX" open-face helmet using the Lombard patent
  • 1955
    • Vincent produces its last motorcycle
    • Yamaha founds motorcycle division and produces the YA-1 "Red Dragonfly"
    • NSU becomes the largest manufacturer
    • 450,000 motorcycles registered in the US
  • 1957
    • Mike Hailwood starts racing
    • Snell Memorial Foundation established to create helmet performance standards
  • 1958
    • Bultaco founded
    • California Highway Patrol motorcycle officers begin using helmets
    • Harley-Davidson introduces rear suspension
    • Over 500,000 motorcycles registered in the US
    • Honda introduces the Super Cub
  • 1959
    • Construction begins on the Daytona International Speedway
    • Vincent goes bankrupt
    • Honda becomes the largest manufacturer
    • Yamaha is the first Japanese motorcycle manufacturer to enter the US market
  • 1961
    • First Daytona 200 on pavement at the Daytona International Speedway
  • 1962
    • Lambretta TV175 Series 3 scooter equipped with front disc brake
    • 646,000 motorcycles registered in the US
    • "You meet the nicest people on a Honda" campaign launched
  • 1963
    • Giacomo Agostini has the first of 122 Grand Prix wins
    • Kawasaki and Meguro merge to form Kawasaki Motorcycle Co
  • 1964
    • Helmets required to have DOT approval
  • 1965
    • 1.4 million motorcycles registered in the US
  • 1966
    • Kawasaki enters the US market
    • Craig Vetter founds Vetter Fairing Co, making the famous Windjammer fairing that influenced the Honda Gold Wing
    • Sears sells Gileras as Sears Motorcycles
    • First ANSI Z90.1 performance standard for motorcycle helmets
    • Highway Safety Act required all the states to have mandatory helmet laws
    • Law directing the issuance of Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standards (FMVSS) enacted
    • Burt Munro makes his under-1000cc world record run of his 1920 Scout Streamliner
  • 1966
    • Ducati under government EFIM management
  • 1967
    • Bell Helmets introduces the "Star" which is the first full-face helmet. It meets Snell criteria and retails for $59.50. It does not have a flip-up visor, as the visor has to be popped out of the rubber surround. The viewport is very small and restrictive.
  • 1968
    • Kenny Roberts starts racing
    • Honda introduces the "Monkey" Z50A Mini Trail 50, which is produced for 50 years until 2017
  • 1969
    • Kawasaki introduces the terrifying H1 Mach-III 60hp 500cc two-stroke triple - the suspension and frame have all the stiffness of a paper towel roll
    • Sears stops selling motorcycles again
    • BSA introduces the Rocket III
    • 660,000 motorcycles sold in the US
    • Stock motorcycles run quarter-mile in less than 13 seconds
    • Honda introduces CB750 Four - first motorcycle with hydraulic disc brakes front and rear - pretty much the first superbike
  • 1970
    • "Easy Rider" released, starring Peter Fonda, Dennis Hopper, and Jack Nicholson
    • 1.1 million motorcycles sold in the US
    • 2.8 million motorcycles registered in the US
  • 1971
    • "On Any Sunday" released, starring Steve McQueen, Mert Lawwill, and Malcolm Smith
  • 1972
    • Suzuki introduces the GT750 Le Mans 70hp 750cc liquid-cooled two-stroke triple also known as the "Water Buffalo" since it was the first Japanese production bike with a liquid-cooled engine
    • The British Government form Norton Villiers Triumph
    • Craig Vetter redesigns the Triumph Rocket III to become the X-75 Hurricane
    • Yamaha Motocross World Championship bikes are equipped with Mono-Shock rear suspension
    • FMVSS 123 standardizes motorcycle controls
  • 1973
    • Honda introduces the Elsinore 250
    • MSF created
  • 1974
    • Honda introduces the GL1000 flat-four Gold Wing - intended to have Vetter Windjammer fairings sold as optional Hondaline accessories, but the molds are accidentally destroyed
    • Suzuki produces the Wankel rotary engined RE-5
    • FMVSS 218, Motorcycle Helmets promulgated
    • 55-mph speed limit established nationwide
  • 1975
    • Giacomo Agostini on a Yamaha is the first two-stroke to win the 500cc Grand Prix. It's his 15th world championship title.
    • Mandatory helmet law revoked
    • 5 million motorcycles registered in the US
    • US traffic fatalities: motorcycle-3,265; passenger car-37,897
  • 1976
    • Honda introduces the two-gear clutchless CB750A Hondamatic
    • Dr. Harry Hurt begins study of motorcycle crashes at USC with NHTSA funding
  • 1977
    • Honda invents the composite Comstar wheel
    • NHTSA funds rear-wheel-steering motorcycle project
  • 1978
    • Kenny Roberts is the first American world Grand Prix champion
    • Norton Villiers Triumph is liquidated
    • Stock motorcycles run quarter-mile in less than 12 seconds
    • VM Group buys Ducati
  • 1979
    • Honda introduces the still naked GL1100 flat-four Gold Wing
  • 1980
    • Honda GL1100I Interstate with full Vetter Windjammer style fairing, saddlebags and trunk is the first Japanese touring bike
    • Kawasaki Z1000 Classic equipped with an electronic fuel-injection system
    • US traffic fatalities peak: motorcycle-5,144; passenger car-27,449
    • BMW R80G/S equipped with a single-sided swingarm
    • First International Motorcycle Safety Conference sponsored by MSF
  • 1981
    • Mike Hailwood and his daughter Michelle are killed by a truck making an illegal turn. The truck driver was fined £100.
    • Yamaha introduces the XS Eleven Venturer full touring bike
    • "Motorcycle Accident Cause Factors and Identification of Countermeasures" (the Hurt Report) released
  • 1982
    • Honda introduces the GL1100A Aspencade, which was even more luxurious
    • Brad Lackey wins 500cc Motocross Grand Prix World Championship with Steve Simons' UDX-60 inverted forks
  • 1983
    • Honda introduces the GL1200
    • Vetter Fairing Co goes bankrupt
    • Triumph Motorcycles goes bankrupt, is bought by John Bloor
    • Stock motorcycles run quarter-mile in less than 11 seconds
  • 1984
    • Kawasaki introduces the GPz-900 Ninja, the first sportbike
  • 1985
    • Freddie Spencer is the only person to win both the 250cc and 500cc Grand Prix World Championships in the same year. He also wins the Daytona 200, Formula 1, and 250cc classes, becoming the only rider to win all three divisions in a single year.
    • Yamaha introduces the V-Max muscle bike, which is produced essentially unchanged until 2008
    • Honda introduces the gold-painted Limited Edition GL1200L with cruise control and PGM-FI fuel injection
    • Suzuki finally introduces their full dress tourer, the GV1400 Cavalcade
    • Suzuki introduces the air/oil-cooled GSXR-750
    • Yamaha TZR250 equipped with the first aluminum beam perimeter frame
    • US traffic fatalities: motorcycle-4,564; passenger car-23,212
    • 5.4 million motorcycles registered and 700,000 sold in the United States
    • Cagiva buys Ducati
  • 1986
    • Suzuki GSX-R750R Limited Edition features a dry clutch
    • Honda CR250 & VFR750F equipped with the first production cartridge forks
    • Lamborghini produces six "Design 90" motorcycles with 1000cc inline-4 Kawasaki engines
    • Superbike ban proposed and defeated
  • 1987
    • Honda introduces the RC-30 homologation special
    • Honda introduces the GL1500 flat-six fully-faired Gold Wing with a "reverse gear" powered by the starter motor
    • Michelin introduces the first production motorcycle radial tires, A59X and M59X
  • 1988
    • BMW K100LT equipped with the first anti-lock brakes - has enormous hydraulic regulators on the swingarm and weighed almost 30lbs
  • 1990
    • Suzuki GSX-R400 & GSXR-1000 equipped with the first production inverted forks
    • US traffic fatalities: motorcycle-3,244; passenger car-24,092
  • 1992
    • The Suzuki GSXR finally becomes water-cooled
  • 1993
    • Ducati introduces The Monster
  • 1994
    • The chain-driven F650 is the first BMW without shaft drive
    • Polaris forms the Victory Motorcycles brand
    • Ducati produces the 916
  • 1995
    • Kawasaki introduces the ZX-7RR limited edition homologation special
    • Maria Costello wins Isle of Man TT and becomes the first woman ever to claim an Isle of Man podium
    • US traffic fatalities: motorcycle-2,227; passenger car-22,423
  • 1996
    • Valentino Rossi starts racing 125cc Grand Prix for Aprilia
    • Suzuki GSXR-750 gets the SRAD ram-air snorkels
    • Texas-Pacific Group buys Ducati
  • 1997
    • Honda introduces the F6C Valkyrie, which is a GL1500 Gold Wing with a higher performance engine in a fairing-less cruiser-style frame
    • USC Head Protection Research Laboratory conducts feasibility study of updating FMVSS 218
    • US traffic fatalities: motorcycle-2,116; passenger car-22,199
    • Organization for Economic Cooperation and Development/Road Safety Committee 9 (OECD/RS 9) Technical Experts Group formed to develop common international methodology for in-depth motorcycle crash investigation
    • MSF and NHTSA sponsor development of the National Agenda for Motorcycle Safety
  • 1998
    • Yamaha introduces the R1
    • Schuberth produces the "System 1" helmet for BMW, which is the first "modular" or flip-up helmet
    • Stock motorcycles run quarter-mile in less than 10 seconds
    • OECD common methodology first used in Bangkok, Thailand
  • 1999
    • US traffic fatalities: motorcycle-2,472; passenger car-20,818
  • 2000
    • Kenny Roberts Jr. wins the 500cc World Championship, making them the only father and son duo to have won the title
  • 2001
    • Valentino Rossi wins the 500cc World Championship on a Honda
    • The Suzuki GSXR-1100 is downsized to the GSXR-1000
    • Honda introduces the GL1800
    • Fabio Taglioni, chief designer of most Ducati motorcycles in the 1950s-1970s, dies at 81
  • 2002
    • Brammo Motorsports founded
  • 2003
    • Yamaha introduces the FJR-1300
  • 2004
    • Honda introduces the Valkyrie Rune, with a springer front suspension
  • 2005
    • Investindustrial Holdings S.p.A. buys Ducati
  • 2006
    • Yamaha releases a limited edition R1-LE/SP in Kenny Roberts "bumblebee" racing colors to celebrate its 50th anniversary. It had front and rear custom Öhlins suspension units developed by the same team as the YZR-M1 MotoGP bike. It also had a slipper clutch and custom forged aluminum Marchesini wheels specifically designed for the LE which shaved nearly a pound off the unsprung weight. Only 500 were made for the US and 500 more for Europe.
    • Honda GL1800 becomes the first production bike with an airbag
    • Yamaha introduces the Gen-II FJR-1300
    • Electricross is founded and produces the "Drift" electric dirt bike
  • 2007
    • Electricross becomes Zero Motorcycles
  • 2008
    • Brammo Motorsports becomes Brammo, Inc and focuses on electric motorcycles
    • Performance Motorcycles S.p.A. buys Ducati
  • 2009
    • Yamaha puts a cross plane crankshaft and big-bang firing order in the R1
    • Yamaha redesigns the V-Max and renames it VMAX
    • Honda introduces the VFR1200A with an automatic dual clutch 6-speed 3-mode transmission
  • 2010
    • Valentino Rossi breaks his leg and misses the first race in his Grand Prix career
    • Zero S begins shipping as the first production electric motorcycle
  • 2012
    • Brammo delivers first Empulse
    • Automobili Lamborghini S.p.A. (a subsidiary of Audi AG) buys Ducati
  • 2013
    • Yamaha introduces the Gen-III FJR-1300
    • “Electric Terry” Hershner becomes the first person to ride cross country on an electric motorcycle, going from San Diego, California, to Jacksonville, Florida, in five days, or 135 hours, with no support and using existing charging infrastructure
    • Zero launches its 102-volt “cellbox” battery architecture and proprietary air-cooled Z-Force motor
  • 2015
    • Victory introduces the uniquely styled Vision tourer
    • Polaris buys Brammo motorcycle business
  • 2017
    • Polaris shutters Victory
    • Nicky Hayden is hit by a car on his bicycle in Rimini, Italy and killed
    • Kenny Roberts sells Hickman, California ranch & training track
    • Honda celebrates producing one hundred million Super Cubs. That's 100,000,000 bikes.
    • Cummins buys Brammo electric powertrain business
  • 2018
    • Honda GL1800 gets a robotically-welded frame and a double wishbone front suspension
