Tire lift tool

I do all the maintenance on my bikes by myself, so I'm at a disadvantage when I'm changing my tires. Normally I need to lift and hold a 30 pound tire & wheel in place accurately enough to slide in the axle, while dealing with spacers, brake brackets, ABS sensors and what-not.

I knocked together a quick hack from pipes and a cheap Chinese lever hoist. It happens to fit on the lift.

This reverses the process, where I'm able to position the bike where I need it to be.

It's pretty sketchy, so I don't leave the bike hanging there long.

Use a front tire chock or rear stand to stabilize things.

Parts list:

  • 2x 6' 3/4" galvanized pipe (uprights)
  • 2x 3/4" galvanized pipe elbows
  • 2x 3/4" galvanized pipe tees
  • 5x 3/4" 18" galvanized pipe (feet and top)
  • 4x 3/4" galvanized pipe endcaps
  • 1/2 ton 5ft aluminum lever hoist (mine was from Toyo-Intl)
  • Short ratchet strap
