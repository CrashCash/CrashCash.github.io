Tire lift tool

I do all the maintenance on my bikes by myself, so I'm at a disadvantage when I'm putting a rear wheel back in place. I need to lift and hold a 30 pound tire & wheel in place accurately enough to slide in the axle, while dealing with spacers, brake brackets, ABS sensors and what-not.

I knocked together a quick hack from the cargo strapping used on semi trailers. The wide strap fits easily under the tire, and the ratchet mechanism holds it and allows fine position adjustments.

This is most useful with a bike lift, but you can use it on the ground, as well.

I bought the main components from Northern Tool + Equipment.

The "Buyers Standard Bolt-On Winch, Model#1903020" and "SmartStraps Winch Strap with Flat Hook-30ftx4in, Model#285" are both $30.

You want the bolt-on winch so you can bolt it to the carrier, and you want the 4" wide strap to better fit under the tire.

Construct an L-shaped carrier from 1x6 pine and corner brackets.

It needs to be at least as tall as the height of the axle on whatever bike stand you're using.

Drill 1/2" holes and bolt the winch to the carrier with 1/2"x1-1/2" bolts and washers.

Make sure the ratchet is facing the correct way up.

Shorten the strap as necessary, and use a flame to melt the end to keep it from unraveling.


I used a long 3/8" ratchet extension as a support rod.

Insert support rod into the passenger footpeg brackets.

You can also put the support rod directly on the swingarm.

Hook the end of the strap over the rod.

Lay the strap under the wheel's path, and move the rest of the tool out of the way.

Put the wheel in place on top of the strap.

Put the tool on lift and tighten the ratchet to take up slack from the strap.

At this point, it should be obvious how you control the positioning of the tire.
